Rules For Businesses in Redwood Heights

General Business Operations▶

1. Registration

*All businesses must be registered and approved by the server’s governing body before operating. This includes providing a valid application with details about the business name, location, and services.

  • Business approval: Ensure all business details are submitted and approved by the server’s governing body before opening.

2. Licensing and Permits

*Businesses must obtain and display all required licenses and permits, including those for specific activities such as selling alcohol, running events, or operating heavy machinery.

  • Proper licenses: Ensure your business has the necessary documentation for specific activities.

3. Compliance with Laws

*Businesses are expected to adhere to all local and server-wide regulations, including tax laws, labor laws, and environmental standards.

  • Adherence to laws: Stay compliant with all legal and server rules to avoid fines or penalties.

4. Hours of Operation

*Businesses must adhere to the hours of operation defined in their application, ensuring fairness and preventing unrealistic practices.

  • Defined hours: Operate your business within the scheduled hours as submitted in the registration.
Fire Safety and Emergency Preparedness▶

5. Fire Safety Equipment

*All businesses must have accessible fire extinguishers, functioning smoke detectors, and visible emergency exit signs.

  • Accessible equipment: Ensure that fire safety equipment is easy to access and in good working condition.

6. Emergency Exits

*Emergency exits must remain unlocked and unobstructed during business hours to ensure safe evacuation in case of an emergency.

  • Unobstructed exits: Keep all emergency exits clear and easily accessible for the safety of staff and customers.

7. Fire Safety Training

*Business owners are responsible for ensuring their staff understands basic fire safety procedures and can respond to emergencies.

  • Staff training: Provide fire safety training to ensure that all staff are aware of emergency procedures.

8. Regular Inspections

*Businesses must allow fire safety officials to inspect their premises regularly to ensure compliance with safety regulations.

  • Cooperate with inspections: Allow fire safety officials to inspect your business premises to ensure compliance with safety standards.
Inspections and Compliance▶

9. Routine Inspections

*Businesses must allow government officials and authorized inspectors to conduct routine or surprise inspections. Failure to comply will result in penalties or possible closure.

  • Cooperate with inspectors: Businesses must allow authorized personnel to inspect their premises to ensure compliance with regulations.

10. Inspection Records

*Businesses are required to maintain up-to-date inspection records, licenses, and permits. These must be available for review upon request.

  • Maintain proper documentation: Ensure all inspection and legal documents are up to date and available for review at any time.

11. Temporary Closures

*If significant safety or compliance issues are found, the business may be temporarily shut down until all issues are resolved.

  • Temporary shutdowns: Businesses must cease operations if deemed unsafe or non-compliant until all issues are resolved and re-approved.

12. Handling Violations

*Violations found during inspections must be addressed within the timeline given by the inspecting authority. Repeat violations may result in permanent business closure.

  • Address violations promptly: Correct any violations within the timeline provided by authorities to avoid penalties or permanent closure.
Rolepaly and Community▶

13. Employee Safety

*Business owners must ensure the safety and well-being of their employees while on duty, including providing fair wages and enforcing roleplay-safe working conditions.

  • Employee welfare: Business owners should provide safe and fair working environments, offering fair wages and maintaining safe practices for all employees.

14. Customer Interaction

*Businesses must provide engaging and realistic roleplay interactions for customers. Poor conduct or neglect of roleplay standards can result in a warning or loss of business privileges.

  • Respectful customer service: All business owners and staff must engage customers with realistic and immersive roleplay to enhance the experience.

15. Collaboration

*Business owners are encouraged to collaborate with other players and businesses to create roleplay opportunities, events, and scenarios.

  • Cross-business collaboration: Working with other players and businesses to generate events or roleplay scenarios helps enrich the server environment.

16. Dispute Resolution

*All disputes involving the business should be handled in-character. If an issue cannot be resolved, seek assistance from server administration.

  • In-character conflict resolution: Any disputes between employees, customers, or other businesses should be roleplayed in-character first.
  • Seek staff support: If disputes escalate beyond roleplay resolution, involve server administration to handle the issue.
Consequences for Non-Compliance▶

17. Penalties for Violations

*Businesses found in violation of these rules may face fines, temporary suspension, or removal from the server, depending on the severity of the infraction.

  • Fines and suspension: Businesses may face penalties such as fines or temporary suspensions if they violate any server regulations.
  • Severe violations: In cases of major infractions, businesses may be permanently removed from the server to ensure fair play.

18. Appeals Process

*Business owners may appeal any penalties or decisions through the server’s designated appeal process.

  • Appeal process: Business owners can submit an appeal to the server’s administration to contest penalties or decisions.
  • Fair review: All appeals will be reviewed fairly, with the final decision resting with the server administration.

*These rules align with the application process and ensure that businesses enhance roleplay while maintaining standards for safety and legality.
