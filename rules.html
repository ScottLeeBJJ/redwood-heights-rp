Redwood Heights Server Rules

1. Respect All Players ▶

1. Respect is a priority.

*Players must treat everyone fairly, regardless of gender, race, or nationality.

  • Redwood Heights is a roleplaying community where respect is a priority. Players must treat everyone fairly, regardless of gender, race, or nationality.
  • No harassment: Avoid bullying, repeated unwanted interactions, or verbal attacks.
  • No hate speech: Discriminatory language based on race, gender, or identity is not tolerated.
  • No toxic behavior: Deliberate aggravation or provocation outside roleplay is unacceptable.

2. Roleplay at All Times (In-Character Behavior)

*Players must stay in character at all times to maintain immersion.

  • OOC Chats: Use out-of-character chat sparingly and mark it with (( )) or use designated OOC channels.
  • Immersion: Avoid references to real-world events or breaking character in-game unless absolutely necessary.

3. No Random Deathmatch (RDM)

*Attacking or killing another player without an in-character reason is prohibited.

  • Conflict escalation: Ensure proper buildup before any violent actions, such as arguments or ongoing tensions.
  • Revenge killing: Do not kill someone for revenge without a justified in-character reason.

4. No Vehicle Deathmatch (VDM)

*Running over or using vehicles to kill players without roleplay justification is not allowed.

  • Accidental VDM: If you accidentally hit another player, roleplay the situation realistically (e.g., call emergency services).

5. No Metagaming!

*Do not use out-of-character information to influence in-game decisions.

  • Knowledge separation: Keep in-game knowledge and out-of-character information separate.
  • OOC voice chats: Don’t use OOC voice chats to relay in-game information.

6. No Powergaming

*Powergaming involves forcing actions onto other players without allowing them to respond.

  • Give others a chance: Allow players to respond to your actions instead of forcing outcomes (e.g., instead of “I knock you out,” say “I attempt to knock you out, what do you do?”).
  • Avoid unrealistic actions: Don’t perform actions that your character wouldn’t be able to do based on their abilities.

7. No Exploiting or Hacking

*Using bugs, glitches, or third-party software to gain an unfair advantage is strictly prohibited.

  • Report bugs: If you discover an exploit or bug, report it to the staff immediately.
  • No external mods: Hacking or using unauthorized modifications will result in an immediate ban.

8. Respect Law Enforcement & EMS

*Law enforcement and EMS play important roles in the server, and their authority must be respected.

  • No cop-baiting: Don’t provoke law enforcement to create chaotic or unrealistic situations.
  • Realistic injuries: If injured, roleplay the effects and engage EMS in the scene realistically.

9. New Life Rule (NLR)

*After death, your character must forget everything leading to their demise.

  • Forget previous events: You cannot seek revenge or return to the scene after dying.
  • Return restrictions: Wait at least 15 minutes before returning to the area and avoid rejoining the same scene.

10. Value Your Life (VLR)

*Your character’s life must be valued in dangerous situations.

  • Fear RP: Show fear when appropriate, such as when threatened with a weapon.
  • Avoid recklessness: Don’t engage in suicidally reckless behavior unless it’s justified by your character’s development.
2. Roleplay at All Times (In-Character Behavior) ▶

11. Realistic Character Development

*All characters must have believable, realistic backstories that justify their behavior, skills, and resources. Avoid creating characters with unrealistic capabilities or behaviors that break immersion.

  • Character skills: Your character should have limitations, based on their profession, education, and experiences. Don’t play a street thug who also happens to be a master hacker without backstory justification.
  • Consistent RP: Stick to your character’s story and don’t shift their personality or behavior drastically without proper narrative buildup.

12. No Griefing or Trolling

*Intentionally ruining another player’s experience for personal amusement, without any roleplay justification, is not allowed.

  • No camping Pillbox: Do not linger around Pillbox Medical Center with the intention of repeatedly targeting or harassing players coming out of the hospital.
  • No trolling: Avoid acting in an obviously unrealistic manner just to disrupt others’ roleplay (e.g., wearing ridiculous outfits in serious situations, running into scenes making random noises).

13. Follow Server Economy Rules

*Any manipulation of the server’s economy, including exploiting jobs, trades, or currency systems, is prohibited.

  • No exploiting job systems: Abusing job mechanics to generate excessive money is forbidden.
  • Fair trading: Avoid exploiting trade systems, such as duping items or money, to gain an unfair advantage.

14. Use Appropriate Names

*All characters must have realistic and lore-friendly names.

  • No joke names: Avoid using names that break immersion (e.g., John Cena, Harry Potter).
  • No offensive names: Character names that are offensive, racist, or suggestive in any way will result in a name change request or further action.

15. Proper Use of Vehicles & Weapons

*Vehicles and weapons should be used within reason, following the roleplay context and your character’s background.

  • No military-grade weapons: Civilians should not have access to military-grade weaponry or high-end vehicles without proper roleplay buildup and authorization.
  • No unrealistic vehicle behavior: Driving vehicles unrealistically, such as off cliffs or through dense traffic at top speeds with no consequences, is prohibited.

16. Do Not Combat Log

*Logging out to avoid consequences such as arrests, death, or any other roleplay scenario is strictly forbidden.

  • Stick with RP: Always finish the roleplay scenario even if it’s not in your favor. Quitting mid-scene is unfair to other players involved.

17. Age Requirement

*The server may have an age restriction to ensure maturity and quality roleplay. All players must be at least 18.

  • Mature behavior: Regardless of age, everyone must act responsibly and handle conflicts or disputes in a mature manner.

18. Respect Staff Decisions

*The server staff work to maintain balance and fairness. All players are expected to follow staff instructions and decisions without resistance.

  • No arguing with staff: If you have an issue with a staff decision, follow the proper appeal process instead of arguing during active scenarios.
  • Staff RP participation: Staff may participate in roleplay scenarios, but their authority within the server is final, regardless of their character.
3. No Random Deathmatch (RDM) ▶

19. No Unrealistic Economy/Wealth Roleplay

*Your character’s wealth should reflect their in-game activities and backstory. Avoid portraying extreme wealth or resources unless it’s consistent with your character’s role and development.

  • Wealth buildup: Your character should not own multiple expensive properties, high-end cars, or luxury items without a realistic in-game explanation.
  • Legitimate gains: All large purchases, such as luxury cars or homes, should have a roleplayed backstory justifying how your character obtained them.

20. No Unrealistic Crime Roleplay

*Criminal activities should be realistic and follow logical progression, especially for new characters.

  • No super-criminals: Avoid roleplaying as a character who can perform every type of crime (e.g., a skilled hacker, street thug, and arms dealer simultaneously).
  • Progression of crimes: Start small (e.g., petty theft) and work your way up to larger crimes (e.g., bank robberies) through proper roleplay interactions and development.

21. No Forced Roleplay Without Consent (Non-Consensual RP)

*Players should never be forced into roleplay scenarios that make them uncomfortable or that involve serious actions like torture, kidnapping, or permanent harm without prior consent.

  • Clear boundaries: Always communicate OOC with the player if you plan to involve them in a scene with sensitive themes.
  • OOC opt-out: If a player feels uncomfortable with a roleplay scenario, they have the right to opt out OOC, and the situation should be rewritten or avoided entirely.

22. Gang and Group Roleplay Rules

*Groups and gangs are welcome in Redwood Heights, but they must follow specific guidelines to ensure fair and enjoyable roleplay for everyone.

  • Gang registration: All gangs must be registered with the server staff, and their actions must align with their registered backstory and goals.
  • Realistic gang behavior: Gangs should avoid unnecessary violence and aim for realistic power struggles, territory control, and interactions with law enforcement.

23. Hostage Situations

*Hostage scenarios can create exciting roleplay but must be executed within specific boundaries.

  • Hostage roleplay guidelines: Hostages must be treated fairly and realistically. Don’t kill hostages without a valid roleplay reason, and give law enforcement time to negotiate.
  • Law enforcement interaction: Allow law enforcement to engage in negotiation tactics. Shooting at cops without any build-up or warning will result in disciplinary action.

24. Handling Long-Term Injuries

*Long-term or permanent injuries should be roleplayed realistically and taken seriously.

  • Medical roleplay: If your character is injured in a major event (e.g., a gunshot wound), you should roleplay the recovery process realistically. This may include downtime, limited mobility, or seeking medical assistance.
  • Permanent damage: In cases of extreme injuries (e.g., losing a limb or eye), make sure your character’s future roleplay reflects these limitations consistently.

25. Respect Public Spaces

*Redwood Heights has a variety of public spaces, and players should treat these locations with respect, following in-character decorum.

  • Public roleplay: When in public places, such as restaurants, parks, or shops, ensure your roleplay fits the setting and tone.
  • No unrealistic actions in public: Avoid large-scale violent acts or chaos in public areas without build-up, as it disrupts others’ roleplay.

26. Staff Assistance and Tickets

*If you encounter issues with other players or experience server-related problems, you can contact staff through the server’s ticket system.

  • Ticket etiquette: Be respectful and provide all necessary details when submitting a ticket to the staff team.
  • Staff authority: Staff decisions are final, and players are expected to follow instructions without argument.
4. No Vehicle Death Match (VDM)▶

27. Respectful Use of Communication

*Communication should be respectful and immersive at all times.

  • In-character voice: Keep voice chat consistent with your character’s tone and personality. Avoid OOC topics.
  • Scenario: During a car chase, remain in character, expressing appropriate emotions like fear or frustration.
  • No spamming: Spamming voice or text chat breaks immersion.
  • Scenario: During a hostage situation, refrain from irrelevant comments that disrupt the roleplay flow.

28. Law Enforcement Roleplay

*Law enforcement should be handled with professionalism, maintaining balance and fairness.

  • Professionalism and authority: Behave as a trained officer would. Use appropriate language and follow procedures.
  • Scenario: In a robbery, approach cautiously, communicate with your team, and try to de-escalate before using force.
  • Use of force: Only use force when absolutely necessary. Deadly force should be a last resort.
  • Scenario: After a shootout, if suspects surrender, restrain them instead of continuing violence.
  • Roleplay consequences: Arrests and investigations should have meaningful consequences for both law enforcement and criminals.
  • Scenario: Process suspects properly after an arrest by reading their rights, collecting evidence, and ensuring realistic consequences.

29. Group Size Limits for Roleplay Scenes

*Roleplay scenes should have balanced group sizes to avoid chaos and ensure fairness.

  • Max group size for hostile scenes: In confrontational scenes like robberies or gang conflicts, groups should not exceed 6 members unless approved by staff.
  • Scenario: Keep participant numbers balanced during a bank robbery to prevent overwhelming the situation.
  • Coordination in large scenes: Manage multiple groups carefully in large-scale roleplays to avoid overwhelming law enforcement or other players.
  • Scenario: In a street brawl, limit the number of participants to maintain balance.
  • Staff intervention: For large events, like gang wars or heists, notify staff in advance to ensure the event runs smoothly.
5. No Robbing Police or EMS ▶

30. No Prop Abuse or Clipping

*Avoid using props or objects in ways that break immersion or give unfair advantages.

  • No clipping: Don’t place vehicles or objects to glitch through walls or barriers.
  • Realistic prop placement: Props like barricades or cones should be used logically, not for trolling or blocking paths unnecessarily.

31. No Unrealistic Escaping or Evasion

*Evasion of law enforcement must be realistic, avoiding superhuman feats.

  • No “superhuman” escapes: Don’t perform unrealistic actions like jumping off buildings without consequences.
  • Lawful arrests: If caught by law enforcement, roleplay the arrest realistically rather than constantly trying to escape.

32. Handling Corruption Roleplay

*Corruption roleplay involving authority figures (e.g., law enforcement) can enhance roleplay, but must be handled carefully.

  • Corruption limits: Major abuses of power need staff approval to ensure fairness.
  • Consequences of corruption: Corrupt actions should lead to realistic consequences like investigations or demotions.

34. Handling In-Game Businesses

*Businesses must follow realistic guidelines and fit into roleplay.

  • Business registration: All businesses must be registered with the staff, including a detailed backstory and operational strategy.
  • No business exploitation: Avoid using businesses for unrealistic financial gain without proper roleplay development.

35. No Griefing at Server Events

*Disrupting server-wide events is prohibited.

  • Respect event organizers: Follow event rules and don’t disrupt them for personal gain.
  • Follow event guidelines: Ensure your roleplay aligns with the event’s guidelines to maintain immersion.

37. Avoiding Conflict Escalation

*Conflicts should aim to create narrative-driven roleplay rather than defaulting to violence.

  • Conflict resolution: Try resolving conflicts verbally before resorting to violence.
  • Respect cooldowns: After a conflict, avoid immediately reigniting it. Let it develop organically over time.

*Disclaimer: All rules listed above are subject to change by the server staff. Players are expected to stay updated on any changes, and failure to follow the rules can result in disciplinary actions.
